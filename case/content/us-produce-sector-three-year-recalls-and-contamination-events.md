- Given that produce often causes the most significant outbreak events (e.g., E. coli, Listeria), the risk to human health and financial exposure is massive.
## __The Incident__
Over the three-year period, fresh produce (leafy greens, salad kits, onions, sprouts, etc.) has been repeatedly subject to recalls due to contamination (pathogens), foreign-material risk, undeclared allergens, and pesticide/residue violations. Each event triggered supply-chain fallout, including product pulled from shelves, distributor disruptions, product destruction, brand damage, possible illness outbreaks, and regulatory action.
## __What Went Wrong__
- Field-level contamination (microbial, chemical, foreign-object) is not caught in real time.
- Harvest, packing, and holding operations lack unified sensor verification and real-time risk validation.
- Supply chain opacity: ingredients or batches move without consistent traceability or anomaly detection.
- Residue/pesticide verification and the mistreatment of “organic/free-range” claims cause integrity issues.
- No single truth-layer to reconcile growing, harvesting, sensor data, packing operations, QA logs, and supplier inputs.
