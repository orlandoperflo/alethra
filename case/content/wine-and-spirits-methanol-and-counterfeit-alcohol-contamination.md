## __The Incident (Three-Year Overview)__
Over the past three years, multiple major methanol poisoning incidents and counterfeit alcohol seizures have occurred worldwide. These events involved tainted vodka, whisky, tequila, and unregulated “home-batch” spirits entering hotels, bars, restaurants, resorts, retail channels, and even duty-free environments.
Common patterns in these events included:
- Methanol is intentionally added to increase perceived potency
- Counterfeit packaging designed to mimic legitimate brands
- Fake tax stamps and forged batch markings
- Supply chains with no verifiable traceability
- Distributors and retailers were unaware they were selling lethal products.
Each incident resulted in widespread illness, hospitalization, deaths, global headlines, and government crackdowns — along with massive consumer anxiety and brand fallout affecting the entire beverage sector.
