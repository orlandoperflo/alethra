## __The Incident__
Tyson Foods initiated a voluntary recall of its “Fun Nuggets” fully-cooked, breaded chicken patties after consumers reported __small metal pieces__ in the product. The 29-ounce bags were produced on September 5, 2023, at a Berryville, Arkansas facility (Establishment P-7211), and distributed across multiple U.S. states, including Michigan, Ohio, California, and others. ([opb](https://www.opb.org/article/2023/11/06/tyson-recalls-30000-pounds-of-chicken-nuggets-after-metal-pieces-were-found-inside/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)) The recall was classified by the USDA Food Safety and Inspection Service as high-risk (Class I) due to the potential for serious health consequences. ([Health](https://www.health.com/tyson-chicken-nugget-recall-2023-8391393?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
## __What Went Wrong__
- Foreign-object (metal) contamination entered the finished product line.
- Real-time detection of small metal fragments failed.
- The production/packaging line lacked a unified verification layer to flag anomalies across sensors, logs, and supplier QA.
