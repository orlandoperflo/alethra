## __The Incident__
Over three years, major U.S. hospital systems documented repeated medication-error incidents resulting from wrong dosage, drug-drug interactions, misadministration, transcription errors, and compliance breakdowns. Cumulatively, these errors resulted in thousands of patient harms—including severe injuries and fatalities—and forced hospitals to absorb billions in additional costs for extended stays, corrective treatments, litigation, and reputational damage.
## __What Went Wrong__
- Medication orders entered incorrectly (e.g., decimal errors, wrong drug)
- Pharmacists and nursing staff lacked real-time cross-verification systems.
- Administration systems were siloed and not integrated into a unified safety layer.
- Vital signs and patient responses were not seamlessly integrated with medication delivery systems, resulting in delayed detection of adverse events.
- No unified “truth layer” to reconcile sensor data, machine logs, human entries, and patient outcomes
