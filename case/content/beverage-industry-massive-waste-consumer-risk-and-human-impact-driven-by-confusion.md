# __The Incident: A Human-Centered Crisis Hiding in Plain Sight__
One of the world’s largest beverage manufacturers suffered a cascading breakdown across three of its flagship products — a flavored water, a sports drink, and a low-calorie juice line.
What started as a routine ingredient substitution led to a catastrophic clarity collapse:
- A natural flavor barrel mislabeled by a supplier caused __allergen cross-contamination__ in a line labeled “Allergen Free.”
- Mislabeled concentrate drums triggered __wrong nutritional panels__ on more than 80 million bottles.
- Scanner conflicts applied sugar-free labels to full-sugar products.
- Sanitation logs didn’t match between shifts — creating a contamination risk the company couldn’t quantify.
- Distribution partners mixed pallets from different batch runs, making traceability for retailers impossible.
The human fallout:
- __Consumer illnesses__, including allergic reactions and GI distress
- __Parents reporting sick children__ who drank mislabeled beverages
- __Diabetics unknowingly consuming full-sugar drinks__
- Retailers pulling all products out of caution
- Consumers losing trust in a brand they grew up with
The company destroyed over __112 million bottles__ because it couldn’t prove what was safe.
