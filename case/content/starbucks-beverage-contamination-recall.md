## __The Incident__
The Starbucks RTD bottled beverage (Vanilla Frappuccino, 13.7 oz) was distributed across retail channels when the joint venture between Starbucks and PepsiCo initiated a voluntary recall after “glass fragments” were found in some sealed bottles. ([Top Class Actions](https://topclassactions.com/lawsuit-settlements/consumer-products/recalls/bottled-starbucks-vanilla-frappuccino-recall-announced-due-to-potential-glass-contamination/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
The recall was classified by the U.S. Food & Drug Administration (FDA) as a Class II event (“may cause temporary or medically reversible adverse health consequences” and “probability of serious adverse health consequences is remote”). ([Top Class Actions](https://topclassactions.com/lawsuit-settlements/consumer-products/recalls/bottled-starbucks-vanilla-frappuccino-recall-announced-due-to-potential-glass-contamination/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
## __What Went Wrong__
- Sanitation/cleaning verification gaps enabled foreign-object ingress.
- Post-fill integrity checks lacked real-time monitoring of glass or foreign fragments.
- Supplier QA data was siloed and not integrated into a unified verification system.
- No real-time correlation across sensor, line-data, and QA logs — leading to unnoticed deviations until distribution.
