# __The Incident__
A major global bottled water supplier — one of the largest in North America — faced a catastrophic contamination and mislabeling event involving several of its best-selling SKUs.
Here’s what happened:
- Spring water and purified water lines were routed through the same system during a maintenance cycle.
- Sanitization logs were mismatched between three shifts, creating false assumptions about what had been cleaned.
- Label rolls for “Purified Water” were incorrectly applied to a run of “Spring Water” bottles due to a scanner routing glitch.
- Microbial contamination (Coliform, HPC) emerged in one batch — but because of misaligned batch codes across co-packers, the company couldn’t isolate which pallets were unsafe.
- Retailers demanded complete shelf removal of all products from the brand, affecting __four SKUs__, __two production plants__, and __over a dozen distribution centers__.
Consumers reported off-taste, sickness, and odor issues — triggering the full-scale recall.
# __What Went Wrong__
This wasn’t a single mistake. This was a __stack of failures caused by confusion__, happening simultaneously:
- Inconsistent batch coding between co-packers
- Missing or unclear sanitation entries
- Misread label scanner data
- No verification layer to detect label-route conflicts
- Distribution databases out of sync with production logs
- Conflicting water-source records between “purified” and “spring” classifications
- Temperature logs lost between warehouse transfers
In the end, the company had no idea what was safe and what wasn’t. So they destroyed everything.
